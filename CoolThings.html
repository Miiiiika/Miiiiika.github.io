Cool Things I Like

Movies

Movies have been important to me for all of my life. The types of movies I like tend to be ones that are experiemental or otherwise weird in nature. They also tend to be slower paced. Here are some of my favorites:

The Grand Budapest Hotel

This is a contender for my favorite movie of all time. It follows the story of a young man named Zero, a migrant from a distant country, who finds work at the Grand Budapest Hotel in a fictional vaguely eastern-European country. I love it for its story, which manages to be both funny, heartfelt, and sad at the same time. The cinematic style is also great. I also absolutely adore its soundtrack.

BARDO: False Chronicle of a Handful of Truths

This movie is a sort-of autobiography directed by Alejandro Inarritu, who is a director from Mexico famous for other movies like Birdman and The Revenant. It is, as the title implies, somewhat falseified, or exaggerated to feel like a more emotional experience. It feels like the movie version of a stream-of-consciousness book. I like it for its emotional storytelling, its masterfully written dialogue, its cinematic style, and I absolutely love the way the ending ties right back in to the beginning of the movie.

Skinamarink

This movie is an expiremental horror film, and one of the only horror movies that has actually creeped me out in a very long time. The plot centers around two young children, maybe 4/5 years old, who wake up to find their parents gone and all the doors and windows to escape from the house in gone. There is no visible monster in the movie. The two children are forced to stick together as the house slowly bends and changes, becoming darker until the only light source is a flickering television screen. The movie is largely composed of still shots of walls, corners, cielings, lit sparsely and with film grain filling the screen. It is relatable to me - the experiences I had of being scared as a child weren't of fantastical monsters chasing after me, but of lying awake at night, too scared to close my eyes, staring at a wall lit only by my computer screen. It is slow, but creepy if you let yourself get into it.

The Revenant

Made by the same director as BARDO, this film follows an American fur-trader and his mixed-race son across the brutal landscape of the American fur trade in the 1840s. The film has amazing cinematography and an equally good soundtrack. The violence in the movie is also particularly well choreographed.

Come and See

This movie was made in the Soviet Union in the 1980s by a director who had lived through the Nazi invasion during the '40s. The movie follows a young boy, Flyora, who joins a partisan group behind enemy lined in occupied Belarus. The movie is very difficult to watch, but I think it is important for that very reason. I have not seen any other movies that so accurately portray the horrors of war, and of the Nazi's invasion in paricular. I wouldn't watch this if you don't want to feel some absolute despair.

The Lord of the Rings

LOTR was one of the most formational series' I watched during my childhood. The Fellowship of the Ring especially is one of, in my opinion, the best movies ever made. The soundtrack especially always gets to me. If you like fantasy and somehow haven't watched this series, I can't recommend it enough.

Cmon Cmon

This movie stars Joaquin Pheonix as a man who finds purpose in his life by caring for his 8 year old nephew. It is extremely heartfelt, and really just a very calm and sweet movie. The soundtrack is also very nice.

Online Media

ENA is a character created by the Peruvian artist Joel G. The world surrounding ENA is very fantastical in a... weirdly digital sense. The whole aesthetic of the series is influenced by old clunky first-person games like the original DOOM, and the visual style of old 90s or present-day vaporwave digital art. I love the series because of its great style and sense of what it is.

Sawah_Draws is an artist who created the characters Jon, Alex, and Marcie, who are all good characters. I love her artstyle. She currently has an ongoing webtoon called Its Kind of a Funny Story.
